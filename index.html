Tribute to A.P.J. Abdul Kalam

About A.P.J. Abdul Kalam

Dr. A.P.J. Abdul Kalam (1931–2015) was an Indian aerospace scientist, visionary leader, and the 11th President of India, widely known as the “Missile Man of India” and the “People’s President.”

Life Style

  • Dr. Avul Pakir Jainulabdeen Abdul Kalam was born on 15 October 1931 in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu.
  • He came from a simple and humble family; his father was a boat owner and imam, and his mother was a homemaker.
  • From a young age, he was hardworking, disciplined, and curious about science and learning.
  • He supported his education by selling newspapers during childhood.
  • He studied Physics at St. Joseph’s College and later Aerospace Engineering at the Madras Institute of Technology.
  • Dr. Kalam believed strongly in education, self-confidence, and moral values.
  • He lived a simple life, remained unmarried, and dedicated himself to the nation.
  • He was known as a great teacher, motivator, and inspirational leader for students.

Achivements

  • Dr. Kalam worked as a scientist at ISRO and DRDO, contributing significantly to India’s space and defense programs.
  • He played a key role in the development of India’s missile technology, earning him the title “Missile Man of India.”
  • He was an important leader behind Agni and Prithvi missiles
  • He contributed to India’s nuclear tests in 1998, strengthening national security.
  • Dr. Kalam served as the 11th President of India (2002–2007) and was known as the “People’s President.”
  • He received many prestigious awards, including:
    • Bharat Ratna
    • Padma Bhushan
    • Padma Vibhushan
  • He wrote several inspirational books such as Wings of Fire, Ignited Minds, and India 2020.
  • He inspired millions of students through his speeches and vision for a developed India.
